This article summarizes the paper, Adequacy-Fluency Metrics: Evaluating MT in the Continous Space Model Framework, 
which is published in 2015 on IEEE/ACM TRANSACTIONS ON AUDIO, SPEECH, AND LANGUAGE PROCESSING, VOL.23, NO.3 by
Rafael E. Banchs, Luis F. D’Haro and Haizhou Li. The authors propose a new automatic machine translation evaluation 
framework, AM-FM, to address the limitations exhibited by both human and automatic evaluation methods.

## Research Problem

1. The authors firstly identified the issues with human evaluation that it suffers from inconsistency problems resulted from both inter- and intra-annotator agreement issues and human evaluation is expensive and time consuming.
2. For current automatic evaluation methods, multiple human-written references are required. The evaluation metrics may be biased towards translations close to references and score good translations differing from the references poorly.

## Motivation

a. Humans can produce absolute evaluations based on more meaningful metrics such as adequacy and fluency [1].
